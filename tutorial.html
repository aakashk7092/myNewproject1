First Aid for Fever

Fever is the body's natural response to fight infections. Follow these steps to manage it effectively:

  • Hydration: Offer plenty of fluids like water or electrolyte drinks.
  • Cooling: Apply a cold compress to the forehead to help lower the temperature.
  • Medication: Use over-the-counter fever reducers such as paracetamol or ibuprofen, if appropriate.
  • Medical help: Consult a doctor if the fever lasts more than 3 days.

First Aid for Burns

Burns can range from minor to severe. Follow these steps for first aid:

  • Cool the burn under running water for 10-15 minutes.
  • Do not apply ice directly to the burn as it may further damage the skin.
  • Cover the burn with a sterile, non-stick dressing or clean cloth.
  • If the burn is severe, seek immediate medical attention.

First Aid for Fractures

Fractures require immobilization to prevent further injury. Here's what to do:

  • Keep the injured limb in the position it was found; do not try to realign it.
  • Apply a splint to immobilize the area, using a rigid object like a stick or rolled-up newspaper.
  • Apply ice packs wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling.
  • Seek medical assistance immediately.

First Aid for Cuts

Cuts can vary in severity. Follow these steps for minor cuts:

  • Wash your hands thoroughly before treating the wound.
  • Stop the bleeding by applying gentle pressure using a clean cloth or bandage.
  • Clean the wound with running water and mild soap.
  • Apply an antiseptic cream and cover the cut with a sterile bandage.
  • For deep cuts, seek professional medical care.

First Aid for Abnormal Heartbeats

Abnormal heartbeats can be a sign of a serious condition. Here's how to respond:

  • Check if the person is conscious and breathing.
  • If they are unresponsive, call emergency services immediately.
  • Perform CPR if the person has no pulse and is not breathing.
  • If they are conscious but experiencing palpitations, help them sit down and stay calm.
  • Encourage them to take deep breaths and avoid exertion.
